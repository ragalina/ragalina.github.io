About Me

I’m a high school student from the Bay Area interested in creating social impact with computer science. I hope to continue learning and working towards creating a more equitable future — one step at a time.

Currently, I serve as the Associate Director of Superposition , where I empower gender-minorities in STEM through hackathons, workshops, and chapters. This is partially fueled by my experiences with Kode with Klossy, where I learned about the need for diversity in tech, and my own experiences with Superposition, which inspired me to pursue computer science. Computer science interests me because of its interdisciplinary nature, and I explore this in my projects — from environmental racism to bioinformatics, through coding, I can explore it all.

Outside of school, I can be found playing badminton, taking a nap with my cat, or reading. My goal for this year is 50 books, and so far, my favorites are Educated by Tara Westover and The Nightingale by Kirstin Hannah! I enjoy traveling — I’ve been to 15 states & 5 countries (and counting!) — and trying new recipes, sometimes successfully. Finally, I love meeting new people, so feel free to reach out!

    • Projects

    Hepatitis C Prediction

    Built 5+ ML models to predict one's likelihood of contracting Hepatitis C, with an accuracy of 99%. Our dataset consisted of blood data and we were the most successful with a Random Forest model. Created as a part of UCLA's Computer Science Summer Institute, Intermediate track.

    Evergreen

    Analyzed effects of climate change on lower-income & racial minority communities with a focus on Superfund sites, lead poisoning, air quality, and heat stroke statistics. We sourced data from the US Census & EPA and used Google Data & SQL. Created as a part of Kode with Klossy Data Science.

    Sorting Hat

    Used data on 4000+ 'sortings' from Pottermore's Harry Potter Sorting Hat Quiz. Cleaned & manipulated data to train a decision tree and XGBoost model, with a final accuracy of 86%.
